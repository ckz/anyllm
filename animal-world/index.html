Lion

Habitat: African Savanna

The lion is one of the largest big cats and a symbol of strength and courage. Lions are the only cats that live in groups called prides, which consist of related females, their cubs, and a small number of adult males.

Interesting Facts:

  • Male lions are easily recognized by their manes
  • Lions can sleep up to 20 hours a day
  • Female lions do most of the hunting for the pride

Emperor Penguin

Habitat: Antarctica

Emperor penguins are the tallest and heaviest of all living penguin species. They are adapted to live in the extreme cold of Antarctica, with multiple layers of scale-like feathers and thick fat deposits.

Bottlenose Dolphin

Habitat: Oceans Worldwide

Bottlenose dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals known for their playful behavior and distinctive "smiling" appearance. They live in social groups called pods and communicate using a complex system of clicks and whistles.

Interesting Facts:

  • Dolphins sleep with one half of their brain at a time
  • Each dolphin has a unique whistle that acts like a name
  • They can swim at speeds of up to 20 mph

Bald Eagle

Habitat: North American Forests near Water

The bald eagle is a majestic bird of prey and the national symbol of the United States. These powerful birds have excellent eyesight and can spot prey from great distances. They build the largest nests of any North American bird.

Interesting Facts:

  • Bald eagles can see 4-7 times better than humans
  • Their wingspan can reach up to 7.5 feet
  • They mate for life and can live up to 30 years in the wild

Capybara

Habitat: South American Wetlands, Rivers, and Swamps

The capybara is the largest living rodent in the world and is native exclusively to South America. These semi-aquatic mammals are highly social and can be found in groups near bodies of water in forests, savannas, and wetlands throughout South America.

Interesting Facts:

  • Capybaras can stay underwater for up to 5 minutes to hide from predators
  • They have webbed feet that help them swim and walk on muddy surfaces
  • Capybaras are herbivores and eat mainly grasses and aquatic plants
  • They are known as "nature's ottoman" because other animals often sit on them

Bengal Tiger

Habitat: Asian Jungles and Forests

The Bengal tiger is one of the largest wild cats alive today. These solitary hunters are known for their distinctive orange coat with black stripes, which provides excellent camouflage in the dense forests and grasslands where they live.

Interesting Facts:

  • No two tigers have exactly the same stripe pattern
  • Tigers can leap distances of over 6 meters
  • They are excellent swimmers and often cool off in lakes and streams
